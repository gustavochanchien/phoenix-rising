About Phoenix Rising

Phoenix Rising brings together people of all ages to marvel at the amazing physics and history behind ballooning in New Mexico as well as to enjoy and learn about the craft of R/C ballooning. Our team members have combied decades of experience flying for educational programs and demos at the Anderson Abruzzo Hot Air Balloon Museum and are always looking to innovate in the field. We follow proper safety protocol, and love to teach others about hot air balloons and let them experience them up close in a small and safe environment. We also love sharing our knowledge on R/C ballooning, how we weave our own R/C gondolas, build our own electronics and telemetry systems, and much more! We perform a lot of demos including:

  • Hands-on activities where we teach you how we prep, launch, and pack up the hot air balloon
  • Live demos to see burners, envelopes, baskets, and radios in action and up close
  • Safe operations led by trained RC balloon pilots and ground crew

Education & Innovation

To us, our R/C balloons are mobile, educational innovation platforms. We work to deliver structured experiences that blend live flight, storytelling, and hands-on STEAM lessons and interactive experiences. We love learning together and developing new systems and programs to get more people involved and in love with ballooning.

  • Live Demonstrations
    We offer engaging, hands-on demonstrations that bring ballooning, physics, and atmospheric science to life. Participants can see real equipment, ask questions, and watch concepts like lift, heat, and airflow demonstrated in real time.
  • Science and Telemetry
    Live science demonstrations plus RC balloon flights for elementary & middle school groups, tying in weather, wind, solar power, flight, and New Mexico science careers. Also advanced demonstrations for adults showing our telemetry technology, data tracking, and experimental innovations.
  • Tissue and Thermal Balloons
    Experience the physics of hot air balloons through tissue and thermal balloon builds. Participants learn about buoyancy, temperature, and airflow, then launch their own creations to see the science in action.
  • Balloon Gondola Weaving
    We weave our own gondolas using traditional techniques. This activity connects craftsmanship, engineering, and cultural history while showing how strength, weight, and design all matter in flight.
  • Balloon Fiesta & Major Festivals
    We regularly fly at Balloon Fiesta and major festivals, demonstrating small and specialty balloons. Our balloons can safely fly in conditions where larger balloons cannot, allowing for unique educational and visual experiences.
  • Ballooning History Lessons
    Explore the history of ballooning, from early human flight experiments to modern scientific and recreational balloons. Lessons highlight key innovations, famous flights, and how ballooning continues to influence science and exploration today.
